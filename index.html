Oil Painting

Tips for practice and improving

Begginer painting

When you first start painting, patience is key to allowing yourself to grow as an artist. Oil painting requires time to dry. This is a plus side because you have the ability to come back and work on your piece overtime before the paint solidifies. You are able to modify and readjust colors where you see fit.

Practice Makes Perfect!

As you become more familiar with materials, solvents, techniques, and the chemistry of the paint, you will begin to formulate your own style. Get Excited! All the hard hours you put in to work on developing skills are now curating your style.

Knowing when to stop

Painting can oftentimes consume you. Overblending can cause your previous layers to lift, rip, and cause unwanted discoloration. It is important to know when you need to step away from an area of the paint, and move on to a different section.
