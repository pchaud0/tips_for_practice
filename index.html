Oil Painting

Step-By-Step Guide : How to Oil Paint

Objective

Learn how to oil paint in this comprehensive guide created by an artist for beginners. Tips include how to clean oil paint brushes and more.

Materials

Paint Brushes

Pallet Knife

Canvas or Gello Board

Solvent, Linseed oil to thin paint

Paper Towels

An Easel

Helpful Resource:

learn the basics of oil painting by understanding the basics

How to Paint in 5 Steps

Step 1 : Gather your Materials

When starting with oil paints, begin with the primary colors; red,blue,yellow, black, and white. These colors will be the foundation to your painting, as you can create any other color in the wheel with these three. If preferred, you may use other colors of paint if you find it easier to use. You can experiment with colors in order to match the colors of your object.

Starter sets provide a good range for beginner oil painters. Most quality paint brushes will specify whether they’re suitable for oil painting.

Because you are a beginner painter, it is important that you choose brushes that are made for oil paints.Typically, these brushes have a different endurance for what types of material they can hold.

For the best surface, you can use a canvas or a primed gesso board to begin painting on.

Step 2 : Set up your work space

Oil paints and solvents come in toxic, and in non-toxic version. It is important to work in a ventilated environment to ensure that the fumes do not harm you or those who are around you. It is best practice to keep the windows open to ensure you are staying safe.

Step 3 : Prime your canvas

Having a primed canvas prepares the surface of where the paint will sit. If the paint does not sit on top of a primed surface, there is a risk of the paint seeping through the painting surface.

Some canvas come pre-primed. If not, you can prime it yourself with Gesso. This can be found in your local art store. Typically, gesso takes 2-3 layers before you can begin painting and need 1-2 hours to dry.

Once primed and dry, you are ready to paint!

Step 4 : Outline your painting

If you have a subject you would like to paint, you can outline your painting with a washed down oil-paint and solvent mixture. This can be done with taking an umber color, mixed with a little bit of solvent, to formulate a consistency that is thinner than the original oil paint.

Some canvas come pre-primed. Begin outlining, or "sketching" the areas of paint you woud like in each section. This gives you an idea of a rought draft as to where you would like colors to lay on the painting surface.

Step 5 : Begin Painting!

After prepping your painting, you’ll need to build up the paint and color. You can start by mixing the paint into the colors that you see in your subject or image. Once done, you are able to lay down each layer of paint in the areas you desire. One of the oil painting basics is to follow the “fat over lean” rule. By doing so, the paint prevents cracking. Continue building up your paint overtime. Ensure that before you place each new layer of paint, the prior layer is dry.

How long does oil paint take to dry? That depends on how thick it is, but it can take three months or longer.
